Escalation Policy

1. When to Escalate
- Issues should be escalated if:
  - The customer is highly dissatisfied or angry.
  - The issue remains unresolved after 48 hours.
  - The request falls outside the agent's authority.

2. Escalation Levels
- Level 1: Supervisor review
- Level 2: Manager intervention
- Level 3: Specialized support team

3. Escalation Process
- Clearly document the issue before escalation.
- Provide all relevant customer interaction history.
- Inform the customer that the issue is being escalated.

4. Priority Escalations
- Immediate escalation required for:
  - Repeated delivery failures
  - High-value orders
  - Legal or compliance-related complaints

5. Customer Communication
- Keep the customer informed throughout the escalation process.
- Provide expected resolution timelines.
- Ensure follow-up after resolution.
